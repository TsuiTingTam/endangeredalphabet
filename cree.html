Cree syllabics are the versions of Canadian Aboriginal syllabics used to write Cree dialects, including the original syllabics system created for Cree and Ojibwe (Cree and Ojibwe). There are two main varieties of syllabics for Cree. Syllabics were later adapted to several other languages.
