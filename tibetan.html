About this projectt

English is the third most spoken native language and the most commonly learnt second language in the world. But there are many more languages. When these dominant languages stretch their hands towards every corner of the world, there are some other languages suffering from extinction at the same time. Focusing on the writings of languages, different endangered alphabets, scripts and spoken languages are collected in this archive.

This project aims to be a conservation archive to document these precious cultural assets. On every refresh of the homepage, one word from the archive will be sourced. Through this random approach, this archive aimed to educate the public and also raise awareness on endangered languages. Short descriptions including meaning of the word, status and origin of the languages are provided on different pages.

The Tibetic languages are a cluster of Sino-Tibetan languages descended from Old Tibetan, spoken across a wide area of eastern Central Asia bordering the Indian subcontinent, including the Tibetan Plateau and the Himalayas in Baltistan, Ladakh, Nepal, Sikkim, and Bhutan. Classical Tibetan is a major regional literary language, particularly for its use in Buddhist literature.
